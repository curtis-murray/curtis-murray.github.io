    Biography:

    Hi, I'm Curtis!

    As a Data Scientist, Mathematician, PhD Candidate, and Energy Market Analyst with five years of experience in Data Science, I am passionate about using my expertise to make a positive impact in the world, addressing a diverse set of critical global challenges from climate change to healthcare.

    Currently employed full time in the energy sector, I am dedicated to developing data-driven strategies for the transition to net-zero sustainable energy systems and climate change mitigation. Concurrently, I am near completion of my PhD in Applied Mathematics at the University of Adelaide. My thesis involves developing and applying Natural Language Processing techniques to social media data to harness patient reported experiences and reveal a patient perspective on healthcare. I am a Research Associate at Swinburne University of Technology, where I use Statistical and Machine Learning techniques to build a model for prediction of elderly falls from an ambient assisted living system. I have also provided value to a diverse team of healthcare researchers by applying natural language processing to analyze adverse event reports. Additionally, I have a strong background in teaching, having worked as a Teaching Assistant for undergraduate and postgraduate courses in mathematics, statistics, and data science, as well as a tutor in first-year mathematics help rooms. In 2019 I graduated with a First Class Honours in Mathematics (Statistics) from The University of Adelaide, and was awarded a University Medal.

    My long-term goal is to collaborate with like-minded individuals and organizations actively seeking to make a positive impact in the world, to create a greener, more equitable future for all. I welcome connections with those who share my vision and wish to discuss innovative approaches to problem-solving

    School of Mathematical Sciences

    The University of Adelaide

    My PhD lies in an intersection of Applied Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science, and Healthcare. Understanding patient experience in healthcare is increasingly important and desired by medical professionals in a patient-centred care approach. Healthcare discourse on social media presents an opportunity to gain a unique perspective on patient-reported experiences, complementing traditional survey data. Harnessing patient reported experiences from this discourse requires modern techniques in Natural Language Processing that can reveal structure from free-text comments. In my PhD I make numerous contributions to this research problem through a series of papers.
